Audi E-Tron GT

2021 — Present  |  Electric performance, no compromise

Overview

The E-Tron GT is Audi's definitive statement on what electric performance can be. It is a four-door Gran Turismo with supercar acceleration, genuine long-distance touring capability, and a design that looks like nothing else on the road. It was developed on the same J1 platform as the Porsche Taycan — the two companies collaborating on underpinnings while developing the final cars independently — and the result is one of the most complete electric vehicles ever built.

Launched in early 2021, the E-Tron GT arrived at a moment when many people still associated EVs with compromise and limitation. Audi set out to destroy that perception entirely.

Performance

The top-specification RS E-Tron GT produces 637 hp in standard form, rising to 646 hp with launch control active. Two electric motors — one on each axle — give the car Quattro all-wheel drive with the ability to vector torque between wheels faster than any mechanical differential could manage. The 0–100 km/h time of 3.1 seconds is achieved in near-silence, with the kind of instant, linear surge that only electric motors can produce.

A second-generation update in 2023 pushed the RS E-Tron GT Performance to 912 hp — briefly making it the most powerful production Audi ever built. That car covers 0–100 km/h in 2.5 seconds.

Charging & Range

The E-Tron GT uses an 800-volt electrical architecture — the same as the Porsche Taycan and a technical leap beyond most EVs, which use 400-volt systems. The advantage is charging speed. At an 800V DC fast charger, the E-Tron GT can add 100 km of range in just five minutes, and charge from 5% to 80% in under 23 minutes. On a full charge, WLTP range is approximately 488 km for the RS variant.

The 800V system also enables a trick called "bank charging" — where two 400V chargers can be combined to deliver 800V charging at locations that don't have dedicated 800V infrastructure. In practice, this means faster charging almost everywhere.

Design

The E-Tron GT's body is long, low, and wide — 4,989 mm from nose to tail, with a roofline that slopes dramatically toward the rear in classic Gran Turismo fashion. Audi's designers were given freedom to create something genuinely dramatic, and they delivered. The frameless side windows, the sculpted bonnet, the full-width light signature at the rear — every detail has been obsessed over.

Unlike many electric cars, the E-Tron GT has no frunk (front trunk) to speak of — the front motor and electronics fill that space. The boot offers 405 litres, plus a further 85 litres in a small front compartment. Practical enough for a genuine road trip.

What It Means

The E-Tron GT is important not just because it is fast or beautiful, but because of what it represents. It shows that electrification and driver engagement are not opposites. You can have instant torque and real steering feel. You can have near-silent cruising and genuine excitement. The E-Tron GT makes the case that the electric future does not mean a boring future — and coming from Audi, the brand that once made the same argument about AWD and performance, that carries real weight.
