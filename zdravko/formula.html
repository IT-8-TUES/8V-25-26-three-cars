Scuderia Ferrari HP

There's no team in Formula 1 such as like Ferrari. They've been there since day one — ever since the first ever World Championship race in 1950 — and they haven't missed a single season since. Founded by the obsessive, temperamental genius Enzo Ferrari in Maranello, Italy, the team carries a weight of history that no other constructor can match. Sixteen Constructors' titles, fifteen Drivers' titles, and a fanbase — the Tifosi — who treat every race like a religious event. Michael Schumacher's five consecutive titles between 2000 and 2004 were almost unreal and nervebreaking to watch, and Niki Lauda's return to racing just 40 days after nearly burning to death in 1976 remains one of the most jaw dropping moments in sporting history. Ferrari isn't just a team. For a huge chunk of the planet, it's a passion that gets passed down through generations.

Mercedes-AMG PETRONAS Formula One Team

If you want to talk about dominance, you have to talk about Mercedes. Eight consecutive Constructors' Championships from 2014 to 2021 — that's not a dynasty, that's something else entirely. Based in Brackley, England, the Silver Arrows built what many consider the greatest technical operation the sport has ever seen, centered around a hybrid power unit that left everyone else scrambling. Lewis Hamilton won six of his seven world titles here, cementing himself as statistically the greatest driver of all time. Mercedes actually have a much older story in F1 — Juan Manuel Fangio drove for them in the 1950s — but it's the modern era that truly defined them. They've had a tougher time since 2022, but nobody in the paddock is silly enough to write them off.

Oracle Red Bull Racing

Red Bull bought a struggling Jaguar team for a reported $1 in 2005 and turned it into the most successful team of the last two decades. That's quite a story. Based in Milton Keynes, England, the team quickly hired Adrian Newey — widely considered the greatest car designer in F1 history — and the results were staggering. Four titles in a row with Sebastian Vettel from 2010 to 2013, then after a difficult middle period, an absolute steamroller of a comeback with Max Verstappen. Their 2023 season was almost uncomfortable to watch — 21 wins from 22 races. Three consecutive Drivers' titles for Verstappen. Red Bull changed what people thought was possible in Formula 1, and they did it with a culture and energy unlike anything the sport had seen before.

McLaren Mastercard F1 Team

McLaren is one of those names that just means something in motorsport. Founded by Bruce McLaren in 1963 — a New Zealander who tragically died testing one of his own cars in 1970 — the team grew into one of the giants of the sport. Their late 1980s period with Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost is the stuff of legend, two of the greatest drivers ever produced pushing each other to the absolute limit, sometimes literally. Senna won three of his titles in a McLaren and the relationship between him and the team was something special. After years of struggle in the turbo hybrid era, McLaren rebuilt quietly and patiently, and it paid off. Back to back Constructors' Championships in 2024 and 2025 with Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri. The papaya is back where it belongs.

Atlassian Williams F1 Team

Williams is the kind of team that makes you believe in the romance of Formula 1. Sir Frank Williams built this team from virtually nothing, running the operation from a phone box at one point early in his career. Based in Grove, England, the team went on to win seven Constructors' Championships and produce six World Champions through the 1980s and 90s. Nigel Mansell, Damon Hill, Alain Prost — the list of legends who raced in a Williams is extraordinary. The darkest day in the team's history came at Imola in 1994 when Ayrton Senna lost his life behind the wheel of their car, a moment that hit the entire sport like a freight train. Years of financial struggle followed Sir Frank's declining health, but the team was sold in 2020 and is now pushing hard to return to the front. The history is there. The hunger is there too.

TGR Haas F1 Team

Gene Haas looked at Formula 1 and decided the smartest way in was to not try to reinvent the wheel. So when his team debuted in 2016 as the first American constructor in decades, they did it by working as closely as the rules allowed with Ferrari, sourcing components and knowledge that would have taken years to develop independently. It raised eyebrows in the paddock, but it worked — they scored points on their very first race. Based in Kannapolis, North Carolina, with their race operations run out of Banbury, England, Haas has always been an outsider in the best possible way. Now rebranded as TGR Haas with Toyota involvement, the team keeps defying expectations. Small budget, big attitude.

Audi Revolut F1 Team

Audi's arrival in Formula 1 has been one of the most talked about stories in the sport for years, and 2026 is when it finally becomes real. The German manufacturer took over the historic Sauber team — a Swiss outfit that had been part of the F1 furniture since 1993 — and spent years quietly preparing their own power unit for the new regulations. Sauber's history is worth knowing too. Robert Kubica's stunning win in Canada in 2008 is one of the great underdog victories, and the team produced countless memorable moments over three decades. Now with Nico Hulkenberg and Gabriel Bortoleto in the cars and a brand new Audi engine under the hood, this is one of the most exciting new chapters the sport has seen in a long time.

Aston Martin Aramco Formula One Team

Aston Martin came back to Formula 1 in 2021 and made absolutely no secret of their ambitions. Lawrence Stroll, the Canadian billionaire behind the project, built a brand new factory at Silverstone that genuinely made other teams nervous just looking at it. The 2023 season gave a glimpse of what might be coming — Fernando Alonso, driving like a man twenty years younger, dragged the car onto the podium time and again with performances that reminded everyone why he's considered one of the all time greats. Then for 2026 they landed Adrian Newey, the designer whose cars have won more World Championships than anyone in history. With Honda power and Newey calling the shots, Aston Martin feel like a team right on the edge of something big.

BWT Alpine Formula One Team

The team based in Enstone has been around in one form or another since 1981, which means they've seen pretty much everything Formula 1 has to offer. They started as Toleman — the team that gave a young Ayrton Senna his first F1 drive in 1984. Then came Benetton, who won back to back titles with Michael Schumacher in 1994 and 1995. Then Renault, who did it again with Fernando Alonso in 2005 and 2006. Then Lotus, then Renault again, then Alpine. Four different names, one factory, an enormous amount of history. The current chapter has been more difficult, but switching to Mercedes power for 2026 and keeping Pierre Gasly alongside Franco Colapinto gives the team reasons for optimism. Enstone has produced World Champions before. Don't rule it out happening again.

Cadillac Formula 1 Team

Cadillac's arrival on the 2026 grid is the kind of story that writes itself. General Motors returning to the pinnacle of motorsport for the first time since the 1960s, riding a wave of American enthusiasm for Formula 1 that honestly nobody could have predicted a decade ago. The journey to get here wasn't easy — the project spent years fighting for approval under the Andretti name before GM and Cadillac stepped in and made it happen. Their base is in Indianapolis, which feels right, with operations also running out of Silverstone. Valtteri Bottas and Sergio Perez are both back on the grid after a year away, two drivers with plenty to prove and a new team with everything to gain. Nobody expects Cadillac to win a title in year one. But then again, nobody expected Red Bull to buy a failing team for a dollar and win four championships either.
