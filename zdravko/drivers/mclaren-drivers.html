Lando Norris

Lando Norris is the reigning Formula 1 World Champion and one of the most popular drivers the sport has seen in years. The British driver joined McLaren in 2019 at just 19 years old and spent years delivering performances that far outpaced what the car was capable of. His breakthrough win came at the 2024 Miami Grand Prix and from there he never looked back, pushing Verstappen hard before ultimately claiming his first title in 2025. Funny, honest, and incredibly quick, Norris represents everything good about the current generation of Formula 1 drivers.

Oscar Piastri

Oscar Piastri arrived in Formula 1 in 2023 after winning F3 and F2 on his debut in each — a record only matched by a handful of drivers in history. The quiet Australian made an immediate impression and by 2024 was regularly winning races. In 2025 he led the championship for much of the season and looked set for the title before Norris reeled him in. The pair have an excellent relationship despite the intense internal competition, and with both signed long term, McLaren have arguably the most exciting driver pairing in the sport heading into 2026.

Driver History

McLaren's driver history is as rich as any team in Formula 1. Emerson Fittipaldi and James Hunt won titles for the team in the 1970s. Then came the golden era — Niki Lauda won in 1984, then Alain Prost claimed three titles in 1985, 1986 and 1989. Ayrton Senna arrived in 1988 and the rivalry between him and Prost became the stuff of legend, with Senna winning three of his titles at Woking. Mika Hakkinen brought back to back championships in 1998 and 1999. Lewis Hamilton won his first title with McLaren in 2008. After years in the wilderness, the team has rebuilt brilliantly with Norris and Piastri now carrying the papaya colours back to the very top.
