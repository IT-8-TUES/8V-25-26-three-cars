Liam Lawson

Liam Lawson has had one of the more dramatic journeys to a full time Formula 1 seat. The New Zealander impressed enormously as a substitute driver in 2023, stepping in for the injured Daniel Ricciardo and immediately looking completely at home. He eventually got his full time chance and showed the kind of grit and pace that suggests he belongs at this level. After being briefly promoted to Red Bull and then sent back to Racing Bulls in 2025, Lawson has used the experience as fuel. He is determined to make 2026 the year he truly establishes himself.

Driver History

As Toro Rosso and later AlphaTauri, this team has been one of the most important nurseries for Formula 1 talent in the modern era. Vitantonio Liuzzi and Scott Speed were among the earliest drivers before a certain Sebastian Vettel arrived and won at Monza in 2008. The list of alumni reads like a who's who of the current grid — Vettel, Vergne, Ricciardo, Verstappen, Sainz, Kvyat, Gasly, Hartley, Albon, Tsunoda, Leclerc. Almost every major team on the current grid has at least one Racing Bulls graduate in their car.
