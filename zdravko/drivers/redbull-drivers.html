Max Verstappen

Max Verstappen is quite simply the best Formula 1 driver on the planet right now. He made his debut aged just 17 with Toro Rosso in 2015, was promoted to Red Bull mid season in 2016, and won on his very first race for the team. Four consecutive World Championships followed from 2021 to 2024, with his 2023 season — 19 wins from 22 races — being arguably the most dominant individual campaign in the history of the sport. Aggressive, fearless, and with a natural feel for a racing car that comes along once in a generation.

Isack Hadjar

Isack Hadjar is one of the most exciting young talents to arrive in Formula 1 in years. The French-Algerian driver impressed massively at Racing Bulls in 2025 before earning his promotion to the senior Red Bull team for 2026. Born in Paris in 2004, Hadjar came through the Red Bull junior programme and showed he had the pace and composure to handle the top team. Now paired alongside the four time world champion, 2026 is his chance to show the world what he is capable of at the highest level.

Driver History

Red Bull have had a remarkable eye for talent since their very first season. David Coulthard and Mark Webber were their early stalwarts, with Webber winning nine races for the team. Then came Sebastian Vettel — four consecutive World Championships from 2010 to 2013, turning Red Bull into the most feared team in the paddock. Daniel Ricciardo brought joy and pace in equal measure from 2014 to 2018. Max Verstappen arrived mid 2016 and took over as the undisputed leader of the team, going on to dominate the sport in a way even Vettel hadn't managed. Red Bull have also used their junior team as a pipeline, promoting Vettel, Verstappen, and now Hadjar through the ranks.
