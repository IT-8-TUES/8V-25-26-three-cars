There's no team in Formula 1 such as like Ferrari. They've been there since day one — ever since the first ever World Championship race in 1950 — and they haven't missed a single season since. Founded by the obsessive, temperamental genius Enzo Ferrari in Maranello, Italy, the team carries a weight of history that no other constructor can match. Sixteen Constructors' titles, fifteen Drivers' titles, and a fanbase — the Tifosi — who treat every race like a religious event. Michael Schumacher's five consecutive titles between 2000 and 2004 were almost unreal and nervebreaking to watch, and Niki Lauda's return to racing just 40 days after nearly burning to death in 1976 remains one of the most jaw dropping moments in sporting history. Ferrari isn't just a team. For a huge chunk of the planet, it's a passion that gets passed down through generations.
