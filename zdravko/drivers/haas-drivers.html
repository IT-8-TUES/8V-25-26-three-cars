Esteban Ocon

Esteban Ocon has had one of the more turbulent careers in recent Formula 1 history. The Frenchman came through the Mercedes junior programme and spent a year on the sidelines in 2019 as a reserve driver before getting his chance at Renault and then Alpine. He won the 2021 Hungarian Grand Prix in one of the most chaotic races in recent memory. After a difficult final season at Alpine, Haas gave him a fresh start in 2025 and he repaid them with consistent points scoring performances. A driver who has always had raw pace — now he needs to show consistency.

Oliver Bearman

Oliver Bearman announced himself to the Formula 1 world in spectacular fashion when he stepped in for the sick Carlos Sainz at Ferrari in Saudi Arabia in 2024, qualifying seventh and finishing seventh on his debut. The British teenager was immediately one of the most talked about young drivers in the sport. Haas gave him his full time debut in 2025 and he delivered — points finishes, impressive qualifying performances, and a maturity well beyond his years. He is one of the most exciting young British talents since Hamilton himself.

Driver History

As a team that only debuted in 2016, Haas don't have the decades of history that other teams carry. But their driver choices have always been interesting. Romain Grosjean and Kevin Magnussen formed a feisty pairing for several seasons, with Magnussen in particular becoming a fan favourite for his no nonsense approach. Mick Schumacher — son of the legendary Michael — raced for the team in 2021 and 2022, carrying one of the most famous names in motorsport. Magnussen returned dramatically in 2022 after a year away and delivered some of his best racing. Now with Ocon and Bearman, Haas have perhaps their strongest driver pairing yet.
