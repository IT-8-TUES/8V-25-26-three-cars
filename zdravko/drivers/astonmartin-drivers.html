Fernando Alonso

Fernando Alonso is simply one of the greatest Formula 1 drivers who has ever lived, and at 44 years old in 2026, he is still competing at the very highest level. The Spaniard won back to back World Championships with Renault in 2005 and 2006 and came desperately close with Ferrari and McLaren in subsequent years. He joined Aston Martin in 2023 and immediately delivered — eight podiums in his debut season that had the entire paddock shaking their heads in disbelief. Now with Adrian Newey designing the cars, Alonso finally has the tools to mount another championship challenge. The hunger has never left him.

Lance Stroll

Lance Stroll has spent much of his Formula 1 career dealing with the complicated narrative of being the team owner's son. The Canadian driver debuted in 2017 and has shown real flashes of ability — a brilliant podium in the wet at Baku in 2017, pole position at Istanbul in 2020, and several other strong results. At Aston Martin he has matured considerably and in 2023 delivered his best season yet alongside Alonso. He will never quite escape the scrutiny, but those who watch closely know there is a genuinely capable Formula 1 driver underneath all the noise.

Driver History

In their previous Formula 1 life in the late 1950s, Aston Martin ran drivers including Roy Salvadori and Carroll Shelby — yes, the same Carroll Shelby who later became famous for the Ford Cobra. When the team returned in 2021 under the Aston Martin name, Sebastian Vettel was the marquee signing, a four time World Champion who gave the team instant credibility. Vettel retired at the end of 2022 and handed the reins to Fernando Alonso, who transformed the team's results overnight. The combination of Alonso's experience and Newey's engineering genius for 2026 is one of the most intriguing storylines in the entire sport.
