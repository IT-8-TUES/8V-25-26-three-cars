Nico Hulkenberg

Nico Hulkenberg is one of Formula 1's great stories of persistence. The German driver has been in and out of the paddock for over a decade, spending years as a reserve driver and making substitute appearances before finally securing a permanent seat again with Haas in 2023. He delivered the performances many always believed he was capable of and earned himself the coveted role of lead driver for Audi's works entry in 2026. A man who has never won a Grand Prix but has never driven anything other than brilliantly, Hulkenberg finally has the platform his talent deserves.

Gabriel Bortoleto

Gabriel Bortoleto is the reigning F2 champion and one of the most exciting young talents to emerge from South America in a generation. The Brazilian won the F3 championship in 2023 and the F2 title in 2024, marking him out as a generational talent. Backed by the McLaren driver programme, he was loaned to Audi — now Sauber — for his Formula 1 debut in 2025 and delivered performances that turned heads across the paddock. In 2026, with a full year of experience under his belt and a works manufacturer behind him, Bortoleto looks ready to make his mark.

Driver History

The team that is now Audi has had a fascinating driver history under the Sauber name. Karl Wendlinger and JJ Lehto were among the early drivers in 1993. The team helped launch the careers of Kimi Raikkonen, who drove for them in 2001 before Ferrari snapped him up. Felipe Massa spent years with the team. Robert Kubica's stunning victory in Canada in 2008 driving for BMW Sauber remains the greatest result in the team's history. Sergio Perez, Nico Hulkenberg, and Valtteri Bottas all raced for the team in various guises. Now as an Audi works outfit, the next chapter of this team's history is just beginning.
